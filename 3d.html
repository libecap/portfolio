3d Model and Animation Study

Scope

The goal and purpose of this project was to learn the fundamentals pf 3d modeling in order to expand my skillset and make me a more marketable graphic designer. For this project I used the package design project I had designed earlier. By creating a 3D model and animation of the package design, this would allow me to showcase the design online to a greater audience who wouldn’t normally be able to physically hold the design (i.e. via social media or portfolio website :) ). This allows me to give the package design a tactile feel digitally.

Research

The 3d software I used is called Blender. It is a free software with powerful rendering capabilities and a variety of functions. It has a reputation for being difficult to learn but with the help of its resource guide and online tutorials I was able to take the diecut of my package design and make into an animated 3d model.

Texture Painting and Materials

{% pattern cards/figure title="Figure header" details="One of the Node trees used to apply materials to the 3d objects" image="/images/node-tree.png" aria-details="node-tree" alt-text="image of a Node tree used in blender to give 3d objects their rendered look"%}
{% pattern cards/figure title="Figure header" details="Texture painting the apple by taking the texture from a photograph of an apple" image="/images/uv-map-texture-paint.png" aria-details="texture-paint" alt-text="Shows a 3d object UV unwrapped with the texture paint of an apple applied to the model's mesh"%}

Texture painting allows you to take the texture of an object from a photo and directly paint it onto the 3d model. For odd shapes UV wrapping is used to flatten the 3d object and make it easier to texture paint. In order to enhance the look created by the texture painting I used material adjustments to give each asset a more photo-realistic look.
