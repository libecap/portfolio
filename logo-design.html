Scope

This is a logo rebrand study I did at Algonquin College. The purpose of the assignment was to go through all the steps of creating a logo for a company.

Research

A logo should aim to be a reflection of a business' passion and cater to their target audience. Before even starting to sketch designs, I needed to learn the core principles of Planet of Sound, the personailty of their customers and who their competitors are. In order to do this, I completed a brand audit, looking into the tone and voice of the business, customer demographics and pyschographics, as well as location of the business and the location of its competitors.

Throughout the sketching phase I explored a variety of concepts, such as planets, audio equipment, and sound frequencies. I also explored different styles such as abstract logo design. Music plays a big role in many people’s daily life. It can get people excited. It can help people relax, focus, day dream, even help their plants grow according to some studies. I wanted the design of the logo to try and capture the dynamic nature of music while also looking professional and high end to reflect the quality of the products sold at Planet of Sound.

Logo Specifications

Since the company needs to communicate on a variety of media sources (i.e. print, web, social media) the logo needs to have some adaptability in order to provide the highest level of visual communication. Variations of the logo have been throughly explored, giving the company flexibility with the logo while maintaining a strong consistent look regardless of where the logo is used.

Colours

{% pattern cards/figure title="Figure header" details="Hex: #62b546" image="/images/colour-green.png" %}
{% pattern cards/figure title="Figure header" details="Hex: #000000" image="/images/colour-black.png" %}
{% pattern cards/figure title="Figure header" details="Hex: #ffffff" image="/images/colour-white.png" %}

Only use the Planet of Sound colour green as an accent. When used as the primary colour, it overpowers the type and the logo. If using the logo on a colourful background, say for packaging, use the inverted logo or the black and white logo. This will create enough contrast for the logo to continue to stand out and be identifiable.

Typeface

{% pattern sections/image-text-inline title="subheader" details="Neue Kabel is the only typeface used with the two weights being bold and regular. The company’s name is split into two different font sizes, using a scale of 2:1 (“Sound” will always be twice the font size of “Planet of”). Bold is used for “Planet of” and regular is used for “Sound” to unite the text and give it a structured and unified look while feeling dynamic." image="/images/planet-of-sound-type.png" %}
