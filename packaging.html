Starbucks Bistro Box

{% pattern images/image3by2 image="/images/packaging-wide.jpeg" %}

Scope

This is a package design I did at Algonquin College. The purpose of the assignment was to find creative ways to reduce the amount of plastic used in packaging, without compromising the product inside or turning off potential customers.

Research

For this project, I chose to redesign Starbuck's Bistro Box. The Bistro Box is a very popular item sold at Starbucks which is made entirely out of plastic. This is for three main reasons:

  • The customers need to see the food
  • The box needs to be recloseable
  • The box needs to be durable to withstand traveling to destinations

When designing a new package, my concept needed to fulfill these requirements as well, with the least amount of plastic as well. Before sketching, I needed to understand Starbucks better as well as their typical customer.

Starbucks Image and Values

Starbucks is an iconic brand recognized all over the world. They started as a small coffee shop on Pike Street in Seattle, Washington growing into what it is today. They can be found in airports, colleges, and in most cities and towns.

Style and image is very important to Starbucks. Everything from the experience in their coffee shops to the packaging of the products they sell. It’s so important that they created a higher end store called Starbucks Reserve. This research tells us that there is a market opportunity at Starbucks for a more environmentally friendly package. Both financially and socially.

Starbucks is a socially conscience company that is willing to incrue higher costs in order to maintain their core values. A couple examples are:

  • One of the first large companies to get LEED environmental certifications.
  • Willing to close serveral stores to retrain their employees in order to maintain core values.

One of Starbucks' goals to be more socially and evironmentally friendly. It is aware of the amount of plastic is uses and actively looks for ways to reduce the amount where it is possible. One way Starbucks is currently doing this is by eliminating straws from their stores. Another possible way they could reduce their use of plastic would be to consider redesigning the Bistro Box.

Outcome

The concept I came up with was a pyramid shaped package. It has an outer container that unfolds into a box tray, allowing the package to be opened and closed. The inner package has food dividers to keep food seperate as well as give the package a more durable design.

It eliminates almost all the plastic used in the current bistro box. It has a plastic window on the outer box to allow customers to see the food quality inside. The package would be made of FBB (folding box board) paperboard. FBB paperboard is an industry standard when it comes to packaging food for its versatility, durability and recyclability.

By printing with vegetable inks, Starbucks can further push the sustainability of the new bistro box.

Strengths of this Design

The design is closer to following Starbucks' design and image asthetics. It can be creatively displayed both vertically and horizontally at starbucks stores. The outer container provides an opportunity for custom designs such as seasonal, specific foods, or special events.

Although the cost of producing this concept box would be more expansive than the cheaper plastic box, these costs could be offset by the marketability of advertising a newer more sustainable bistro box which falls in line with their sustainability efforts.

Only use the Planet of Sound colour green as an accent. When used as the primary colour, it overpowers the type and the logo. If using the logo on a colourful background, say for packaging, use the inverted logo or the black and white logo. This will create enough contrast for the logo to continue to stand out and be identifiable.
